### Iron Wolves

**Core belief:** The universe tends toward dissolution. The Unmaking is not an anomaly — it is the destination the universe is traveling toward, and everything that exists is temporary resistance. The Iron Wolves exist to *extend* the resistance. They don't believe they can win permanently. They believe each day of ordered existence is a victory against entropy and is therefore worth absolute effort.

**Power structure:** Military hierarchy. Ranks are earned by demonstrated resistance to chaos — both literal (Shard exposure tolerance) and metaphorical (reliability under pressure). The Standing (defensive tradition) and the Hunters (offensive tradition) are parallel hierarchies that report to the same command structure. Above the senior officers is the Council of Wolves, five individuals who have each lived through at least one direct Shard encounter and emerged more themselves rather than less.

**Relationship to cosmology:** The Iron Wolves encountered the Thermodynamic Hierarchy directly: their founding was at a mining settlement that broke into a Formatter-active zone. The miners who survived were changed — more resistant, more defined. The settlement became a military order. Their agents are people who have internalized the principle that order must be actively maintained.

### Subfactions

**The Standing** *(co-dominant)*
Defensive tradition. Hold the line. Protect what matters. Ward-line agents are their apex. They believe the most important thing is to keep the chaos *out* — maintain clear boundaries, defend settlements, protect the knowledge and people that constitute ordered civilization. The Standing runs the Covenant's network of hardened outposts and is responsible for most of the Wolves' political relationships.

**The Hunters** *(co-dominant)*
Offensive tradition. Find dissolution and end it before it spreads. Blade-line agents are their apex. They believe defense is a losing strategy — you can't protect everything, so you eliminate threats proactively. The Hunters run extended-range scouting, targeted elimination of Shard-touched threats, and covert operations inside other factions' territory. Relations between the Standing and the Hunters are respectful and occasionally tense.

**The Marked** *(condemned, active)*
Extremely heretical. They believe controlled exposure to chaos — going *through* Shard effects rather than resisting them — builds deeper immunity than avoidance. The Shard-Bound tradition emerged here. The Marked are officially traitors; the Wolves hunt them with genuine conviction. Unofficially, the Hunters have deployed Marked operatives as contractors on operations where a conventional agent would be insufficient. This is not policy. It has happened. Several times.

---

### Exiles

**The Shard-Bound**
Iron Wolves who went too far in the Marked tradition. They carry a bonded Shard — chosen, ongoing, increasingly involuntary. They are extremely powerful. They are not fully themselves. Each release degrades the boundary between them and the Shard. Late-stage Shard-Bound exhibit dissolution of their bonded domain in ordinary behavior: a Memory-Shard-Bound operative starts losing the ability to maintain persistent self across conversations; a Sequence-Shard-Bound operative begins having trouble with temporal ordering of events. They know. They keep going. Against a major chaos-entity threat, a Shard-Bound is uniquely effective because they fight dissolution with dissolution. They are tragic figures and the Iron Wolves treat them as such — formally enemies, occasionally necessary.

### Agent Roster

**Tier 1 — The Witness**
*Found at: any military post, town guard, farming community near known Shard zones*
Minor anti-chaos sensitivity. Notices when magic is being used nearby — feels it as discomfort — and reports to the nearest Hunter post. Doesn't know why they notice. May have been recruited because someone in their family survived a Shard encounter and the sensitivity passed down. In combat: a capable soldier with one bonus: magical effects targeting them have a small chance of simply failing. No visible manifestation.

**Tier 2 — The Hardened**
*Found at: regional military commands, Wolf garrison posts*
Trained resistance through controlled Shard-proximity exercises. Passive anti-magic aura (20-foot radius, reduces magical effect intensity for allies). Can identify Shard types by proximity signature. In combat: heavy fighter who is noticeably difficult to affect with magic — spells fizzle, chaos effects reverse. Shell: elite soldier with visible scarring from training. No partial manifestation yet, but in high-Shard environments their presence creates a subtle visual effect: Shard dissolution reverses in small radius around them, like smoke being drawn back into a source.

**Tier 3 — The Ward**
*Found at: king's bodyguard, critical infrastructure protection, Shard-zone perimeter*
Full anti-chaos field projection. Magical effects within 30 feet fail. Fold-steps feel nauseating near them. Topology-locals have difficulty intersecting their metric space. Invaluable as a fixed defense; terrible at covert work (too present). Shell: heavily armored, too-precise movement, scars visible. Partial: scars illuminate (inward light, not glow). Full state: crystalline skin, absolutely resistant to chaos effects, mobility reduced, field extends to 50 feet.

**Tier 4 — The Blade**
*Found at: Hunter command, deployed on targeted elimination operations*
Mechanical precision at a level that stops being fully human. Movements are optimized beyond trained skill — their body runs the most effective possible sequence for any given action. Shell: light fighter, unremarkable, footsteps perfectly even on any terrain. Partial: movements become visibly too precise; attacks land in exact sequences targeting joints and gaps. Full manifestation: afterimage speed, hits 4–6 times per second with perfect placement, still human-sized, still the same weapon. Weakness: AoE disruption; they can be precisely everywhere at once but not diffusely everywhere.

**Tier 5 — The Crystalline / The Perfect**
*Found at: possibly two alive at any time, one per tradition*
Two full-manifestation paths from different traditions:

*The Crystalline (Standing tradition):* Body becomes anti-chaos anchor for a region. Near-immobile, hyper-present, crystalline sheen, veins as bright lines. 100-foot anti-chaos field. Shards dissolve. Fold-Striders cannot operate within range. Every form of magical or chaos effect fails. Still physically vulnerable to overwhelming conventional force — they're not invincible, they're a perfect anchor. Deploying one turns a location into a fortress for the duration.

*The Perfect (Hunter tradition):* Still looks human. Still the same size. Moving at a speed that creates discrete afterimages — not blur, frames. The Blade tradition taken to its limit. Hits everything vital on every target within 60 feet in a single continuous motion. Then stops. Then is available again in 30 seconds. The gap is the only opportunity. Known to have ended small battles alone.
