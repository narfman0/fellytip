### Deep Tide

**Core belief:** Space is not fixed. The world has more rooms than it shows you. The fold — the warped geometry left by the Kindled's Sealing — proved that topology is manipulable, and what can be manipulated can be navigated. Movement is the highest value. A Tide operative who is stuck is dead, professionally and often literally.

**Power structure:** Relatively flat compared to the Covenant. Cells organized by specialization (trade, intelligence, assault) report to regional Captains, who report to the Admiralty (a council of seven, all former field operatives). The Admiralty doesn't command — it coordinates. Tide culture rewards competence and independence; a Cell that solves its problem without asking for help is celebrated. A Cell that asks for help and still fails is reassigned to maritime logistics.

**Relationship to cosmology:** The Deep Tide discovered parallel topology through coastal cave systems near certain geological formation — places where the fold's edge gets thin enough that experienced navigators could sense the geometry shift. They've been quietly exploiting this for 200 years. Their agents learn to enter the fold partially, then fully, then to navigate it. The deeper the fold-access, the less the agent is fully located at any given coordinate.

### Subfactions

**The Navigators** *(dominant)*
Practical. They use fold-access for competitive advantage in trade and intelligence work and don't think too hard about what it means. The fold is a tool. A Shard is a hazard to be logged and avoided. The Sunken Realm is interesting from a navigational-geometry perspective and terrifying from every other. Navigators are the majority and the public face of the Tide.

**The Walkers** *(philosophical minority, respected)*
They believe all topology-travel is accumulating toward something — a destination that exists in no current topology but that sufficient fold-traversal approaches. Not teleologically (they're not sure there's intent), but mathematically: the manifold of all possible fold-paths converges on something. What that something is, they're working on. Walkers are the Tide's theorists and are disproportionately represented among the highest-tier agents because their understanding of fold-geometry is deepest.

**The Drowned** *(heretical, dangerous)*
They believe the fold has a *current*. Like the ocean, it moves, and you can either fight it or let it carry you. Deliberately surrendering navigation to fold-current produces vastly faster travel and access to fold-regions the Navigators can't reach — at the cost of destination certainty. You end up where the current goes. Drowned operatives are the most mobile agents in the world and the least predictable. The Admiralty officially condemns the practice because of the agents who let the current carry them and didn't come back. Unofficially, they deploy Drowned agents for missions where destination is less important than arrival.

---

### Exiles

**The Elsewhere**
Operatives who entered the fold too many times, stayed too long, or followed the Drowned practice to a point of no return. They haven't died — they exist. But they exist *sideways* to normal reality. Slightly translucent when not attending to their own solidity. They can appear anywhere a fold-seam runs, which is many places. They're unsettling to be around (the fold-presence creates low-level spatial unease in others). The Tide doesn't employ them formally but uses them as contractors for specific insertion jobs. They don't seem to mind. They seem to have stopped minding things in general.

### Agent Roster

**Tier 1 — The Drifter**
*Found at: ports, markets, border crossings*
Knows one small fold-entry: a specific wall in a specific building that they can step through to emerge 15 feet away on the other side. They use it for smuggling. They may have discovered it accidentally. The Tide identifies them through their trading patterns and recruits them. Shell: indistinguishable from any dock worker or merchant. The tell: they occasionally go somewhere without using the door and seem surprised when this surprises you.

**Tier 2 — The Smuggler**
*Found at: trade networks, urban cells*
Trained fold-navigation for routes up to 200 feet. Can move goods or people through topology shortcuts, pass through guarded checkpoints, and exit a building from a different floor than they entered. Used for logistics, courier work, and light infiltration. In combat: avoids it; uses fold-step to break engagement. Shell: merchant's agent or trade factor. Partial: briefly translucent during a fold-step.

**Tier 3 — The Unseen**
*Found at: courts, military command, high-value targets*
Full infiltration capability. Extended fold-presence (up to 10 minutes partially in fold, emerging to strike and retreating). Identity flexibility via fold-assisted physical restructuring (limited; they can maintain one alternate appearance for days before it degrades). Can pass through any wall once per 30 seconds. Shell: looks exactly like someone who should be here — and they've done the research. Partial: becomes translucent and hard to hit; fold-presence visible as spatial blur. Full state: three simultaneous instances, only one primary.

**Tier 4 — The Tide-Walker**
*Found at: assault operations, breaching missions, naval command*
Heavy combat with spatial distortion. Uses fold-steps to close distance instantly and attack from angles that don't geometrically correspond to their position. Shell: heavy fighter in maritime-pattern armor with geometric inlay. Partial: inlay activates, spatial distortion visible, fold-steps in combat. Full manifestation: drags a visible fold-rift behind them; directional defense meaningless; attacks arrive from behind regardless of facing.

**Tier 5 — The Nowhere**
*Found at: possibly three or four alive; deployed for existential-tier operations*
A person who has become a trajectory rather than a position. Their body still exists but is not committed to a single location. Multiple simultaneous instances, each equally real, each capable of independent action. In communication with each other (they are each other). The full manifestation is still humanoid — five figures moving in loose formation, each a complete person — but they share one intention. Environmental: their presence creates low-level spatial instability in a 60-foot radius; fold-entries that aren't theirs open unpredictably nearby.
