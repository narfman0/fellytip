### Ash Covenant

**Core belief:** The Weave is divine residue — the sediment of an impossibly vast primordial consciousness that became the physics of this region of the universe. Magic is not technology; it is *communication* with something that mostly forgot it exists. The Covenant exists to regulate that communication, prevent another Unmaking, and guard the boundary between safe magic and the depths that swallowed the Kindled.

**Power structure:** A formal religious hierarchy — Listeners at the base (who detect Weave disturbances), Wardens in the middle (who enforce), Seers at the upper echelon (who predict and advise), Unravelers at the combat apex, and the Ordained (full manifestation, possibly one alive at any time). The Covenant has significant political power because kings want Seers as advisors and Wards as protection.

**Relationship to cosmology:** They experience the Weave as divine. Their agents tap into its substrate — first to perceive it, then to briefly rewrite it. The deeper the access, the less the agent resembles someone praying and the more they resemble someone being answered.

### Subfactions

**The Orthodoxy** *(dominant)*
Magic is sacred and must be regulated. Unlicensed Weave-access is heresy because it risks another Unmaking. The Orthodoxy maintains the licensing apparatus, monitors Shard drift, and dispatches Wardens to suppress wild magic. They believe the Kindled fell because they accessed the Weave without reverence — treating the divine as engineering.

**The Whisperers** *(tolerated minority)*
Heretical view: the Weave is *actively* speaking, not passively receiving. They believe a Warden who only listens for danger is wasting the connection. Whisperers attempt to hold extended conversations with the Weave and report back what they receive. The Orthodoxy tolerates them because their reports are sometimes accurate and occasionally crucial. They tolerate the Orthodoxy because the institutional structure provides cover for their deeper experiments. Whisper-trained Seers are notably more powerful than Orthodoxy-trained ones — and considerably less stable.

**The Sunken Scholars** *(condemned, active underground)*
Extremely heretical. They believe the Kindled were the closest any civilization has come to understanding the Weave fully, and that condemning them as a cautionary tale is destroying the most important body of knowledge in history. Sunken Scholars attempt to translate Old Auremn, make contact with Revenants, and recover Kindled research. The Orthodoxy hunts them. They're right that Kindled knowledge is valuable. They're wrong that they can handle it safely.

---

### Exiles

**The Unbound**
Covenant agents who went too deep into Weave-perception and could not return to normal cognition. They didn't go mad — they went *elsewhere*. They wander, sometimes helping, sometimes incomprehensible. An Unbound will walk into a town, address the local lord by name before meeting them, solve a problem no one asked them to solve, and leave. They're considered holy by some villages and dangerous by the Orthodoxy (who officially classify them as "suffering from Weave-saturation"). They occasionally recruit: someone who shows natural Weave-sensitivity and isn't already in the Covenant system.

### Agent Roster

**Tier 1 — The Listener**
*Found at: every village with a Covenant presence, which is most of them*
Officially a local priest. Actually an early-detection node for the Covenant's Shard-monitoring network. Listeners have one ability: they feel Shard drift before it's visible. They don't know why their headaches align with strange local events. Some of them understand what they are; many don't. When activated, they send word to the nearest Warden post. In combat: not built for it. Minor aura that makes chaos effects slightly less effective in their presence.

**Tier 2 — The Warden**
*Found at: regional centers, trade routes, anywhere magic is practiced*
Trained Weave-sensitivity. Can identify unauthorized magic use and Shard signatures. Has a passive suppression field (10-foot radius) that reduces magical effect intensity. Carries a weapon that has been Weave-keyed to disrupt channeled magic. In combat: mid-tier fighter with anti-magic utility. Shell: looks like a lawman or soldier with distinctive Covenant sigil-work. Partial: sigils activate, suppression field becomes visible as faint distortion around them.

**Tier 3 — The Seer**
*Found at: king's courts, military command, Covenant leadership*
Full predictive Weave-access. Perception extends through walls and ahead by seconds. Marks targets with Weave-signatures that Wardens can track. Invaluable as intelligence assets — they know what's coming. In combat: avoids direct engagement; repositions constantly, always slightly out of reach. Shell: court advisor who seems preternaturally well-informed. Partial: eyes white, moves ahead of events. Full state (rare): floating, wrapped in Weave-thread, outcomes in range pre-determined for 6 seconds.

**Tier 4 — The Unraveler**
*Found at: deployed against specific threats, rarely seen in peacetime*
Heavy combat agent. Anti-magic suppression at range, weapon strikes that bypass magical defense by targeting the target's Weave-pattern rather than their body. Slow and methodical. Shell: armored knight with sigil-work. Partial: sigils ignite (dull gold, not fire), magic fails around them. Full manifestation: Weave-thread wrapping, physics distortion in range, fires die, gravity unreliable. Mobility reduced — becomes an area of effect, not a mobile threat.

**Tier 5 — The Ordained**
*Found at: possibly one alive at any time; their existence is not publicly acknowledged*
Full Weave-manifestation. The person is still present but the Weave has reciprocated the connection fully. Floating, radiant, perceives in all directions simultaneously. Their attacks are technically certainties — the outcome is determined before the motion. Environmental: a 100-foot radius where physical law is slightly negotiable (in the Covenant's favor). Deploying one ends wars. The Covenant has deployed one three times in recorded history. Each time ended with significant collateral and a Seer assigned specifically to monitor the Ordained for signs of becoming an Unbound.
