### Merchant Guild

**Core belief:** Reality is information. The Weave maintains a self-model of the world — a running simulation of what everything is and what happened. Most people interact with the world; the Guild interacts with the record. If you control what the record says happened, you control what happened. They don't fear the Recursion Problem. They're the only faction that sees it as an opportunity.

**Power structure:** Deliberately opaque. Public-facing structure: licensed trade houses, arbitration courts, banking. Actual structure: a network of Cells with specific information mandates, coordinated by a body called the Principals (who the Ledger faction believes doesn't exist, and the Principals allow this belief to persist). Above the Principals, possibly, is someone called the Accountant — a title, not a name. Whether the current Accountant is one person or several operating in rotation is a question the Guild considers proprietary.

**Relationship to cosmology:** The Guild discovered the Recursion Problem through an unexpected route: they were in the business of records, ledgers, and contracts — precision documentation of what happened. Several generations of particularly precise record-keepers noticed that the world's own record sometimes disagreed with Guild records, and that Guild records occasionally won. They developed this into a discipline. Their agents are people who have learned to maintain near-perfect consistency between their presented self and their actual self — and who have learned to deliberately break that consistency as a weapon.

### Subfactions

**The Ledger** *(dominant public face)*
Pure information brokers. They don't take sides; they sell information to all sides and believe this neutrality is their greatest protection. A war is good for the Ledger as long as both sides are buying. They are formally apolitical. This is not cynicism — they believe that once the Ledger starts choosing sides, it becomes a faction, and factions get destroyed. Ledger agents carry information for hire and maintain strict protocol about not acting on what they know.

**The Principals** *(dominant actual power)*
They believe the Guild should be actively shaping events, not merely recording them. They use intelligence to steer history toward outcomes that expand Guild influence, prevent catastrophic instability (bad for trade), and gradually move the world toward conditions they prefer. The Principals consider the Ledger useful as cover and as intelligence infrastructure. They consider themselves realists. The Ledger considers them a corruption of the Guild's original mandate (if the Ledger knew they existed, which officially they don't).

**The Hollow Market** *(heretical, feared even within the Guild)*
They believe the Weave's self-model can be *written to*, not just read. Not merely maintaining consistency — actually editing the record. Making the world remember something differently. The Hollow Market has allegedly successfully altered the outcome of three historical events. These claims are unverified because if the editing worked, the evidence would be in the edited record. The Principals are deeply ambivalent: the power is real and useful and completely uncontrolled, with failure modes that include localized reality dissolution. The Hollow Market is tolerated as long as they don't edit anything the Principals have a stake in.

### Exiles

**The Recursive**
Calibrated agents who became too deeply inconsistent. They lost the ability to maintain any stable identity at all. They cycle through faces, mannerisms, and apparent histories in ways that have no pattern. They're not lying — they're just all of their past identities simultaneously and none of them consistently. They drift through cities leaving confusion in their wake: people remember meeting them differently, records of their presence contradict each other, witnesses give incompatible descriptions. Some of them can be temporarily stabilized by extended contact with a Tempered Iron Wolf (the Ward presence forces a consistency collapse). They find this extremely distressing.

### Agent Roster

**Tier 1 — The Counter**
*Found at: market stalls, court kitchens, dock warehouses, anywhere information moves*
Remembers everything. Trained to retain and report: prices, names, faces, conversations. Has a modest ability to be unremarkable — not invisibility, just the practiced art of being forgettable. The Weave-aspect is minimal: their recursion signature is very slightly suppressed (they present no inconsistency, so they register as background). The Covenant's Wardens don't notice them. In combat: not built for it. Will not be found in combat by choice.

**Tier 2 — The Assessor**
*Found at: trade factors, arbitration courts, minor diplomatic missions*
Trained minimal-recursion maintenance. Can sustain two distinct identities with separate behavioral profiles without degrading either. Has a passive read on Weave-divergence: can sense when someone near them is highly inconsistent (Shard-touched, Fold-Strider mid-fold, heavily stressed). Used for authentication and infiltration of mid-tier targets. Shell: trade representative. Tell: they ask questions that are too precise for casual conversation — they know what answer they're looking for.

**Tier 3 — The Mirror**
*Found at: royal courts, military command, Covenant hierarchy, wherever high-value targets operate*
Full infiltration capability. Long-duration identity maintenance (months). Leaves no Weave-trace. Invisible to Seer perception (no signature to track). Can sustain a full cover identity including social relationships, professional expertise, and personal history. Shell: whoever they need to be. Partial manifestation (under combat stress): face flickers through 2–3 identities rapidly; gains evasion from uncertainty of position. Full state: 4–5 simultaneous identity-instances, all real, recursive fog in 40-foot radius.

**Tier 4 — The Auditor**
*Found at: Guild Principals, critical operations, strategic deployment*
Prediction through recursion-consistency analysis. They observe the divergence between what the world's self-model predicts will happen and what actually happens, and triangulate from the difference. This produces near-perfect short-term prediction. In combat: sidestepping instead of dodging (already standing where the attack isn't). Issues commands that are always correct. Shell: quietly impeccable in everything. Partial: eyes become reflective, movements become pre-emptive. Full state: 30-second perfect prediction window; immune to damage during it; afterward, briefly exhausted.

**Tier 5 — The Hollow**
*Found at: one, possibly; the Guild deploys this with extreme reluctance*
Full recursion manifestation. A person who has become the Weave's self-model interacting with the world directly. They can *rewrite* local history — cause the world's record to say something different than what happened, and have the world adjust accordingly. Small-scale, short-duration, enormous personal cost. Full manifestation is a humanoid figure made of overlapping possible pasts and futures simultaneously, cycling through what was and what might have been. Environmental: in their 80-foot radius, records are unreliable, memory becomes negotiable, and cause/effect relationships occasionally run the wrong direction. The Guild has deployed one Hollow in recorded history. The records of that deployment are inconsistent.
